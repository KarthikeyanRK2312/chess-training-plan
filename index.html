7-Day Chess Training Plan

Day 1 – Foundation

Goal: Warm-up, set a baseline, and learn basic tactical themes.

  • Lichess.org → Do 20 puzzles (accuracy > speed).
  • Watch Hanging Pieces & Forks tutorial on Lichess Learn.
  • Play 2 rapid games (10+0) on Lichess — review mistakes using analysis board.

Day 2 – Pins & Skewers

Goal: Recognize attacking lines and overloaded pieces.

  • Lichess Puzzle Streak — aim for 10 correct in a row.
  • Study Pins & Skewers at Chessable Free Tactics Course.
  • Play 1 blitz (5+0) & 1 rapid (10+0) — identify missed tactical shots after.

Day 3 – Double Attacks & Discovered Attacks

Goal: See multiple threats at once.

  • Lichess puzzles with "double attack" filter.
  • Watch Discovered Attack lesson on YouTube.
  • Play 2 blitz games (5+0) — focus on spotting discovered checks.

Day 4 – Checkmates in 1–3

Goal: Build quick calculation speed.

  • Puzzle Storm (3 mins) — repeat 3 times, beat your score.
  • Practice Checkmate Patterns on Chess.com.
  • Play 1 rapid game (15+10) — after each move, ask: “Do I have mate threats?”

Day 5 – Strategic Thinking

Goal: Go beyond tactics into plans.

  • Read "How to Think in Chess" basics (PDF summary online).
  • Watch Strategic Concepts on Lichess.
  • Play 1 long game (30+0) — think about pawn structure, activity, king safety.

Day 6 – Endgame Tactics

Goal: Learn key winning techniques when fewer pieces remain.

  • Lichess Endgame Training (rook vs pawn, K+P vs K).
  • Chessable → Checkmate Patterns Manual first chapters.
  • Play 2 blitz games — deliberately trade into won endgames.

Day 7 – Mixed Test Day

Goal: Apply everything learned.

  • Puzzle Racer — 5 rounds.
  • Play 3 games (blitz & rapid) — apply at least one tactic per game.
  • Review all games — note recurring mistakes.
Tip: Keep a notebook with you and write 1 thing you learned each day. After 7 days, you'll have your own mini strategy guide.
